  \begin{block}{Implications \& Recommendations}

    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Default-off templates:} public authorities should adopt centrally maintained web templates with third-party trackers disabled by default and explicit, documented opt-in for additions.
      \item \textbf{Procurement safeguards:} contracts should mandate full visibility into third-party components, documented data flows, and a standing right to remove non-essential trackers.
      \item \textbf{Privacy-preserving analytics:} prioritize self-hosted or log-based analytics with strict retention limits, plus periodic automated audits of official domains for third-party requests.
      \item \textbf{User-side protection:} citizens can block third-party cookies, use tracking protection or dedicated browser profiles for government tasks, and limit ambient permissions and extensions.
    \end{itemize}

  \end{block}
