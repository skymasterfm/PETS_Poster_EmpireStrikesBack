  \begin{block}{Key Findings}

    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{50\%} of government websites embed a tracker by 2025, up from near zero in 1996; steep post-2016 surge.
      \item \textbf{Third-party dominates:} 48\% of sites carry third-party trackers vs.\ only \textbf{4.4\%} first-party.
      \item \textbf{Highly uneven adoption:} Singapore (\textbf{95.5\%}), Australia (\textbf{89\%}), Bangladesh (\textbf{82.5\%}) lead; China (\textbf{6.1\%}), Germany (\textbf{9.2\%}), France (\textbf{11.6\%}) persistently low.
      \item \textbf{Concentrated ownership:} Google embedded \textbf{68K times} (Meta: 6,635); \emph{google-analytics.com} and \emph{googletagmanager.com} present in \textbf{all 61 countries}; mean $HHI_{\mathrm{org}}$ = \textbf{0.601}.
      \item \textbf{Tracker gaps:} \textbf{39 gaps} across \textbf{26 countries}; 37 cases suggest deliberate temporary suspensions.
      \item \textbf{High churn:} only \textbf{30\%} of trackers persist year-over-year; avg.\ \textbf{77 new uptakes/year} globally.
    \end{itemize}

  \end{block}
